Responsible Social Media Use – Checklist 

Do’s:
Use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication
Think before posting, ask yourself if it’s respectful and appropriate
Adjust privacy settings to control who can see your posts and information
Verify information from reliable sources before sharing
Maintain a professional and positive online presence
Credit original creators when sharing content
Be mindful of your digital footprint — posts can stay forever
Use social media for learning, networking, and skill-building
Report suspicious or harmful content to platform authorities
Take regular breaks to avoid overuse and maintain mental well-being

Don’ts:
Don’t share personal or sensitive information (address, phone number, passwords)
Don’t post offensive, abusive, or inappropriate content
Don’t engage in cyberbullying, trolling, or spreading hate
Don’t accept requests from unknown or fake accounts
Don’t overshare your location or daily routine publicly
Don’t spread rumors, fake news, or unverified information
Don’t post when emotional or angry, it can lead to regret
Don’t plagiarize or use others’ content without permission
Don’t click on suspicious links or downloads
Don’t ignore your academic responsibilities due to social media use
